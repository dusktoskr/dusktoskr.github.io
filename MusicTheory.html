Cantus Firmus

Fixed Voice Rules

  1. 8-16 notes long
  2. No rhythm
  3. Begin & end on tonic
  4. Approach final note as 2-1 or 7-1
  5. All melodic consonances
  6. Range no greater than a tenth
  7. Single climax
  8. Clear logical connection and smooth shape
  9. Stepwise motion with small leaps
  10. No repeated motifs
  11. No consecutive leaps in the same direction
  12. Large leaps must resolve by a step in the opposite direction
  13. Leading tone goes to tonic

Seventh Chords

  • major seventh: major triad with a major seventh (maj7 or △7)
  • dominant seventh: major triad with a minor seventh (7)
  • minor seventh: minor triad with a minor seventh (m7)
  • diminished seventh: diminished triad with a diminished seventh (dim7 or °7)
  • half-diminished seventh: diminished triad with a minor seventh (⦰7)

Roman Numeral Analysis

  1. If a chord is in inversion, stack it in root position (preferably mentally).
  2. Identify all the notes of the chord, taking its quality into account.
  3. Write the Roman numeral that corresponds with the scale degree of the chord’s root, reflecting the quality in the Roman numeral (uppercase or lowercase).
  4. Write any additional quality symbols ( °, ∅, +) as needed.
  5. Write figures after the Roman numeral.

Non-Harmonic Tones

Embellishing tones

  1. Passing Tone (PT)
    • Preceeded and resolved stepwise in the same direction
    • E -D -C
  2. Neighbor Tone (NT)
    • Step to a note and back again
    • C - B - C
  3. Appogiatura (APP)
    • A Jump, then a step in the opposite direction
    • C - A - B
  4. Escape Tone (ET)
    • A Step, then a leap in the opposite direction
    • C - B - E
  5. Suspension (SUS)
    • Stays the same, the resolves down
    • D - D - C
    • 9-8 suspension
    • 7-6 suspension
    • 4-3 suspension
    • 2-3 suspension
  6. Retardation (RET)
    • Stays the same, then resolves upward
    • C - C - D
  7. Anticipation (ANT)
    • Step, then stays
    • C - D - D
  8. Pedal Tone (PED)
    • Stay Reguardless of other notes
    • Usually the bass
    • Rachmaninoff Piano Concerto 2
  9. Changing Tone (CT)
    • Double Neighbor Tone
    • Go to both neighbor tones and back
    • C - D - B - C
